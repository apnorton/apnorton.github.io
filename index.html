About Me

Hi! My name is Andrew Norton, and welcome to my (perpetually under construction) website. I am currently a third-year student at University of Virginia pursuing a BS in Computer Science. My primary interest is the relationship between computer science and mathematics, with a special focus on machine learning and artificial intelligence. This is particularly applicable to my background in competitive robotics.

I am currently the president of ACM@UVa, the school's chapter of the Association for Computing Machinery. Through this organization, I competed in the International Collegiate Programming Contest (ICPC). This past November, the team consisting of myself and two other UVa students placed 1st out of over 180 teams in the ICPC Mid-Atlantic Regional Competition, earning us a spot in the World Finals, which will be held in Phuket, Thailand in May of 2016. My passion for competition programming led me to choose Computer Science as a major; I regularly participate in HackerRank competitions and participated in USACO during my senior year of high school.
